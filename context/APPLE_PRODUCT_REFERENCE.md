## Key Notes for Schematic Hunting

1. **820-xxxx board numbers** are the #1 identifier for Mac schematics. One A-number model can have multiple board revisions (e.g., A1398 = 820-3332, 820-3662, 820-3787, 820-00138, 820-00163, 820-00426).

2. **Board number format changed ~2015**: 4-digit (820-3662) → 5-digit (820-00138). Full format: `820-XXXXX-A` (revision letter).

3. **iPhone schematics** are identified by codename (D-series, N-series) rather than 820 board numbers.

4. **Apple Silicon Macs** (M1+) have board numbers but schematics are much harder to find publicly.

5. **Model number regex**: `A[123]\d{3}` matches all Apple A-numbers (A1278 through A3xxx).

6. **EMC numbers** (EMC 2835, EMC3178, etc.) are another common identifier in schematic filenames.
